Your goals!

Welcome to where you set your goals!

We can control our body composition through calories and macronutrients - called "macros" here.

If you want to lose weight, you just need to eat less than you spend (also known as being in "caloric deficit").

If you want to gain weight/muscles, the opposit: eat more than you spend (a.k.a "caloric surplus").

And you control that by setting a caloric expense goal!

You can automatically calculate yout caloric expense or you can set it manually - which is useful for weight loss diets. After weeks of caloric deficit, our body tends to keep decreasing our caloric expense, so you can manually decrease it here.

Surplus or Deficit?

Don't go too hard on surplus and deficit! Our body reacts better to slower transitions. Keep the surplus or deficit to a maximum of 500 kcal a day.

Set your macros:

And here are our macronutrients! There are three kinds of macros: proteins, fats and carbohydrates (carbs).

You can research macro strategies for yourself, but a good starting point is 2 grams of protein per body weight kg and 0.8 grams of fat per body weight kg. Carbs could start from 2 grams (weight loss) to 4 grams (weight gain).
